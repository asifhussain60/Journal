## 1. PRE-TRIP TIMELINE

### 6-8 Weeks Before
- Check passport expiration (many countries require 6+ months validity beyond travel dates)
- Research visa/ETA requirements (UK ETA required for Americans since Feb 2025; EU ETIAS launching 2026)
- Schedule vaccinations (some need multiple doses or 4-6 weeks to take effect)
- Book flights (3-6 months ahead for best international fares; Sundays cheapest to book, Wed/Thu cheapest to fly)
- Start travel insurance shopping

### 4 Weeks Before
- Purchase travel insurance (buy within 1-2 weeks of first booking for best coverage)
- Book accommodations and major activities
- Notify bank/credit cards of travel dates and destinations
- Check health insurance coverage abroad (US Medicare/Medicaid do NOT cover international)
- Research local laws, customs, dress codes, tipping norms
- Register with STEP (Smart Traveler Enrollment Program) for US citizens

### 2 Weeks Before
- Confirm all reservations (hotels, restaurants, tours, transport)
- Make copies of passport bio page (one with trusted person at home, one in carry-on, one in checked bag)
- Download offline maps and translation apps
- Check weather forecasts, pack accordingly
- Arrange pet/house care, mail hold, bill payments

### 1 Week Before
- Online check-in for flights (24 hours before)
- Final packing: chargers, adapters, medications, comfort items
- Print or save digital copies of all confirmations
- Share full itinerary with emergency contact at home
- Check destination for any new travel advisories
- Fill prescriptions; carry medications in original labeled containers

### Day Before
- Charge all devices
- Confirm ride to airport / check parking
- Set out-of-office if needed
- Final weather check for packing adjustments

## 2. INTERNATIONAL TRAVEL ESSENTIALS

### Documentation
- Passport: valid 6+ months beyond return date
- Visas/ETAs: check specific country requirements (changes frequently)
- Travel insurance policy documents
- Vaccination records (COVID, yellow fever, etc. as required)
- Copies of all documents (physical + digital/cloud)
- Emergency contacts list including embassy/consulate info

### Flight Booking Intelligence
- Best price window: 3-6 months before departure (~10% savings at 6 months)
- Best day to book: Sunday (up to 15% savings)
- Cheapest flying days: Tuesday-Thursday
- Seat selection: book early for couples to sit together
- Layover minimum: 2 hours domestic, 3 hours international
- Arrive at airport: 3 hours before international departures

### Health & Medical
- US Medicare/Medicaid have ZERO international coverage
- Minimum recommended coverage: $50,000 emergency medical, $100,000 medical evacuation
- Medical evacuation can cost $20,000-$200,000+ without insurance
- 1 in 4 Americans experience a medical issue while traveling abroad
- Some medications legal in the US are illegal in other countries -- research this
- Carry medications in original pharmacy-labeled containers
- Pack a basic first-aid kit: pain relievers, antihistamines, anti-diarrheal, bandages

### Money & Banking
- Notify bank/credit cards of travel plans
- Get cash from ATMs at destination (NOT airport currency exchange -- bad rates + high fees)
- Carry two different credit/debit cards from different banks as backup
- Research tipping norms for destination
- Know the exchange rate before you go
- Consider a no-foreign-transaction-fee credit card

### Communications
- Activate international roaming or buy local SIM/eSIM
- Download offline maps (Google Maps allows offline areas)
- Save key phrases in local language
- WhatsApp/Signal for free international messaging
- Know emergency numbers for destination (not always 911)

### Jet Lag Management
- East-to-west travel: stay up later in days before departure
- West-to-east travel: go to bed earlier in days before departure
- Rule of thumb: 1 day of adjustment per time zone crossed
- First 2-3 days: plan lighter mornings, expect early waking/evening fatigue
- Stay hydrated, minimize alcohol on flight, get sunlight at destination

## 3. ROAD TRIP & DOMESTIC PLANNING

### Driving Limits & Pacing
- Maximum recommended: 8 hours driving per day (~500 miles at highway speed)
- Take 15-minute breaks every 2 hours
- Ideal daily target: 4-6 hours (300-400 miles) for enjoyable pace
- Account for 20-30% more time in metro areas (traffic, parking, navigation)

### Route Planning
- Prioritize scenic routes over highways when schedule allows
- Plan one "anchor" attraction per day plus 1-2 flexible stops
- Research route-specific concerns: mountain passes, construction zones, toll roads
- Gas stations: never let tank drop below quarter in rural areas
- Apps: Google Maps for navigation, Roadtrippers for attractions along route, GasBuddy for fuel prices

### Vehicle Preparation
- Full service: oil, tires (including spare), brakes, fluids, wipers
- Emergency kit: jumper cables, flashlight, blankets, water, basic tools
- Phone mounts, chargers, aux/Bluetooth for audio
- Cooler with snacks and drinks to reduce fast-food stops

### Accommodation Strategy
- Book anchors (first and last night, key destinations) in advance
- Leave 1-2 nights flexible for spontaneity
- Highway-adjacent hotels for early departures; downtown for exploration nights
- Check-in times: most hotels 3-4 PM; plan accordingly

### Scenic Drives Best Practices
- Research sunrise/sunset times for optimal lighting and photo ops
- Scenic drives best early morning or golden hour (fewer crowds, best light)
- Pull over safely for photos -- never stop on the road
- Research seasonal closures (mountain passes, park roads)

## 4. HALAL & DIETARY TRAVEL

### Restaurant Research
- Apps: Zabihah (largest halal restaurant directory), HalalTrip, Muslim Pro, HappyCow (vegetarian/vegan options)
- Research halal options BEFORE arriving at destination
- In areas with limited halal options: seafood and vegetarian are always safe fallbacks
- Many upscale restaurants accommodate halal requests with advance notice

### Accommodation Considerations
- Hotels with kitchen/kitchenette enable self-catering when halal options are scarce
- Extended-stay suites (like Home2 Suites) offer full kitchens
- Research nearby mosques or prayer rooms
- Some hotels provide prayer mats and Qibla direction on request

### Emergency Food Strategy
- Pack halal-friendly snacks for travel days, layovers, and road trip stretches
- Instant oatmeal, nuts, dried fruit, protein bars as backup
- In non-halal areas: grocery stores with international sections often carry halal products
- Fast-food chains with halal options vary by country -- research specific locations

### Meal Planning for Trips
- Map halal restaurants along the route BEFORE departure
- For road trips: identify halal-friendly stops every 2-3 hours
- For international: download HalalTrip guides for destination cities
- Reserve halal restaurants for dinner (most important meal); lunch can be flexible

## 5. BUDGET PLANNING & COST ESTIMATION

### Daily Budget Tiers (per person, US destinations, 2025-2026)
- **Budget:** $80-120/day (modest hotels, casual dining, free/low-cost activities)
- **Mid-range:** $150-250/day (3-star hotels, nice restaurants, paid attractions)
- **Comfortable:** $250-400/day (4-star hotels, fine dining, premium experiences)
- **Luxury:** $400+/day (luxury hotels, high-end dining, VIP experiences)

### Budget Category Breakdown (typical split)
- Accommodation: 30-40% of total
- Dining: 25-30% of total
- Transport (gas, tolls, parking, rideshare): 15-20%
- Activities & attractions: 10-15%
- Shopping & miscellaneous: 5-10%

### Cost-Saving Strategies
- Book flights on Sundays, fly midweek
- Stay outside city centers (10-30% cheaper)
- Eat big lunches (lunch menus cheaper than dinner), light dinners
- Research free/low-cost days at museums and attractions
- Use gas apps (GasBuddy) and toll calculators
- Grocery store breakfasts save $15-25/day per person vs. restaurant

### Budget Buffer
- Add 10-20% buffer to total estimate for unexpected costs
- Separate emergency fund: $300-500 for genuine emergencies
- International trips: add 5% for currency fluctuation and ATM fees
- Road trips: add fuel cost buffer for detours and traffic

### Per-Day Estimation Template

## 6. COUPLE TRAVEL & PACING

### Itinerary Architecture
- **Days 1-2:** Calm, settling in. Adjust to location, light exploration. Combat jet lag.
- **Days 3-5:** Peak energy. Schedule major attractions, day trips, adventures.
- **Days 6-7:** Wind down. Relaxation, shopping, revisiting favorites.
- **Final day:** Nothing ambitious. Pack, farewell meal, easy transit.

### The 60/40 Rule
- Plan 60% of your time, leave 40% unstructured
- One "anchor" event per day (big activity or dinner reservation)
- Morning: planned activity OR rest (never both rushed)
- Afternoon: flexible exploration or downtime
- Evening: dinner reservation (the one non-negotiable)

### Preventing Burnout
- Never schedule strenuous activities on consecutive days
- Build in one full "rest day" per 4-5 days of travel
- After a big event (party, long drive, heavy sightseeing): next morning is recovery
- Spa/pool/beach days serve double duty: romantic AND restful
- Let the less-energetic partner set the pace

### Couple-Specific Tips
- Discuss must-dos vs. nice-to-dos BEFORE the trip
- Allow solo time (30-60 min): coffee alone, journal, call home
- Avoid over-optimizing every meal -- some can be spontaneous
- Designate a "navigator" and a "vibes person" for road trips
- End each day with something pleasant (dessert, walk, hotel time) not logistics

## 7. DAY TRIP & WEEKEND GETAWAY

### Day Trip Radius
- Ideal day trip: destination within 1-2 hours one way
- 2-3 hour drives are doable but eat into the day significantly
- Account for return drive fatigue (especially after a full day)
- Plan the furthest point first, then work your way back toward home

### Weekend Getaway Structure
- **Friday evening:** Arrive, check in, easy dinner nearby
- **Saturday:** Main event day -- full but not frantic
- **Sunday:** Relaxed morning, one light activity, depart by 2-3 PM to beat traffic

### Seasonal Considerations
- Spring (Apr-May): gardens, tulip festivals, waterfalls at peak flow, pleasant hiking
- Summer (Jun-Aug): beaches, outdoor festivals, long daylight hours, heat management
- Fall (Sep-Nov): foliage drives, harvest festivals, apple/pumpkin farms, cooler hiking
- Winter (Dec-Mar): holiday markets, ski trips, indoor attractions, shorter days

## 8. SAFETY & EMERGENCY PREPAREDNESS

### Before You Go
- Register with STEP (Smart Traveler Enrollment Program) for US citizens
- Share full itinerary with trusted contact at home
- Research nearest US embassy/consulate at destination
- Save emergency numbers: local police, ambulance, embassy, insurance hotline
- Photograph all documents; store in secure cloud folder

### On the Ground
- Keep valuables in hotel safe, not in bags
- Use hotel address card in local language for taxi drivers
- Avoid displaying expensive jewelry/electronics in unfamiliar areas
- Know nearest hospital/clinic at each stop
- Keep emergency cash ($100-200) separate from main wallet

### Travel Advisories
- Check travel.state.gov for destination advisories before and during trip
- Level 1 (Exercise Normal Precautions) through Level 4 (Do Not Travel)
- Most travel insurance excludes Level 3-4 destinations
- Sign up for embassy alerts for your destination

## 9. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### Planning Mistakes
1. **Over-scheduling:** Trying to see everything; leads to exhaustion and resentment
2. **Ignoring transit time:** Not accounting for getting between attractions (parking, walking, traffic)
3. **Skipping travel insurance:** One medical emergency abroad can cost $50,000+
4. **Last-minute passport check:** Discovering expiration 2 weeks before travel
5. **Not researching visa requirements:** Being denied boarding at the airport
6. **Booking non-refundable everything:** Plans change; flexibility has value
7. **Ignoring jet lag:** Scheduling important activities on arrival day

### Financial Mistakes
1. **Airport currency exchange:** Worst rates and highest fees
2. **Not notifying banks:** Card frozen mid-trip for "suspicious activity"
3. **No budget tracking:** Spending blindly then getting a shock at the end
4. **Overestimating what you'll do:** Buying attraction passes for 10 things; doing 4

### Health Mistakes
1. **Assuming US insurance works abroad:** It almost never does
2. **Not carrying prescription info:** Medications confiscated at customs
3. **Skipping vaccinations:** "It won't happen to me" -- until it does
4. **Dehydration:** Especially on flights and in warm climates

### Couple-Specific Mistakes
1. **One person plans everything:** Both should have input and buy-in
2. **No downtime:** Every moment scheduled; no room to breathe
3. **Hangry arguments:** Always have snacks; never skip meals for sightseeing
4. **Comparing to Instagram:** The trip is yours; stop benchmarking against strangers
